Non-invasive Deep Tissue Neuromodulation via Ultrasound-Sensitized Neurons

S. Rodriguez, M. Chen, L. Thompson et al. â€¢ Published in Nature Biotechnology 2024

Abstract

Sonogenetics is a transformative technology that allows for the non-invasive control of cellular activity using ultrasound. By genetically engineering neurons to express ultrasound-sensitive ion channels, such as Piezo1 or MscL, we can achieve high-precision neuromodulation deep within the brain without the need for invasive implants or cables. This study demonstrates the efficacy of acoustic reporter genes (ARGs) in tracking neuronal activation in real-time using functional ultrasound (fUS) imaging.
